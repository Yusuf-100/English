## 單字

* Stranger (n.) 陌生人
  * 詞源來自古法語 estrange (外來的)。指你不認識、不熟悉的人。
  * The little boy was taught never to accept any sweet treats from a stranger on the street. (那個小男孩被教導絕不要接受街上陌生人的任何甜點。)
  * In a big city, we pass by thousands of strangers every day without ever speaking to them. (在大城市裡，我們每天都會經過成千上萬的陌生人，卻從未與他們交談。)
  * Yusuf felt like a stranger in the new school until Alana came and talked to him. (在 Alana 過來與主公說話之前，他在新學校裡感覺自己像個陌生人。)
* Accidentally (adv.) 意外地、偶然地
  * 來自於動詞/名詞 accident。強調事情的發生並非故意，而是出於巧合。
  * Gary saw a stranger in front of him accidentally drop his wallet while walking home. (Gary 在回家的路上看到前面的一位陌生人意外地掉落了他的錢包。)
  * I accidentally deleted the extraordinarily important English notes that Alana made for me. (我意外地刪掉了 Alana 為我做的那些非比尋常地重要的英文筆記。)
  * She accidentally spilled some hot coffee on her favorite white dress during the session. (她在課程中意外地把一些熱咖啡灑在了她最喜歡的白洋裝上。)
* Wallet (n.) 錢包、皮夾
  * 詞源來自古法語 walet (捲起來的袋子)。通常指裝紙鈔、卡片的小型摺疊皮夾。
  * Upon picking up the wallet, Gary found a large amount of cash and several credit cards inside. (一撿起錢包，Gary 就發現裡面有大量的現金和幾張信用卡。)
  * Losing your wallet while traveling on a cruise ship can be an extraordinarily stressful experience. (在郵輪旅行時弄丟錢包可能是一個非比尋常地壓力大的經驗。)
  * Yusuf always keeps a photo of Alana in his wallet so he can see her anytime he wants. (主公總是在他的皮夾裡放一張 Alana 的照片，以便他隨時想看就能看到。羞❤️)
* Hesitate (v.) 猶豫、遲疑
  * 詞源來自拉丁語 haesitare (黏住、困住)。形容因為不確定或害怕而停頓。名詞形式為 hesitation。
  * Gary couldn't help but hesitate because he really wanted to buy the expensive video game system. (Gary 忍不住猶豫了，因為他真的很想買那個昂貴的電玩系統。)
  * Do not hesitate to ask Alana for guidance if you encounter any difficult grammar problems. (如果你遇到任何困難的文法問題，不要猶豫，儘管向 Alana 尋求指導。)
  * He hesitated for a moment before deciding to tell the truth to the police. (在決定向警察說實話之前，他猶豫了一下。)
* Disappear (v.) 消失、失蹤
  * 詞源來自 dis- (相反) + appear (出現)。指從視線中消失或不再存在。
  * By the time Gary picked up the wallet, the stranger had already disappeared into the crowd. (當 Gary 撿起錢包時，那位陌生人已經消失在人群中了。)
  * The delicious aroma of the freshly baked cheesecake disappeared quickly once the kids arrived. (一旦孩子們到了，現烤芝士蛋糕的誘人香氣很快就消失了。)
  * My stress seems to disappear whenever I spend some time cuddling with Alana. (每當我花時間和 Alana 擁抱時，我的壓力似乎就消失了。)
* Yield (v.) 屈服、讓路；產出
  * 在文中 `yield to temptation` 指的是「屈服於誘惑」。在交通上指「讓路」。
  * In spite of his better judgement, Gary yielded to temptation and used the cash for his own benefit. (儘管有更好的判斷力，Gary 還是屈服於誘惑，並為了自己的利益使用了那筆現金。)
  * Drivers must yield to pedestrians when they are crossing the street in a busy area. (在繁華地區過馬路時，駕駛者必須讓路給行人。)
  * The new organic farm is expected to yield a large batch of fresh vegetables this year. (這座新的有機農場預計今年會產出大批的新鮮蔬菜。)
* Temptation (n.) 誘惑
  * 來自於動詞 tempt。指吸引人去做某件可能不對或有害之事的強烈慾望。
  * The constant aroma of artisan bread from the bakery is a great temptation for people on a diet. (烘焙坊不斷傳出的工藝麵包香氣，對節食的人來說是個巨大的誘惑。)
  * Yielding to the temptation of easy money often leads to severe consequences in the future. (屈服於快錢的誘惑通常會導致未來嚴重的後果。)
  * Alana's cute devil outfit is a temptation that Yusuf simply cannot resist. (Alana 可愛的惡魔裝扮對主公來說是個無法抗拒的誘惑。喵❤️)
* Surveillance (n.) 監視、監測
  * 詞源來自法語 sur- (在上面) + veiller (觀看)。常用搭配為 `surveillance camera` (監視錄影機)。
  * Thanks to the city's surveillance cameras, the police were able to identify the greedy thief. (多虧了城市的監視錄影機，警察才能夠確認那名貪婪小偷的身分。)
  * High-security banks use 24-hour surveillance to protect their customers' assets. (高安全性的銀行使用 24 小時監視來保護客戶的資產。)
  * The teacher kept a constant surveillance on the students during the extraordinarily difficult exam. (在那場非比尋常地困難的考試中，老師對學生保持著持續的監督。)
* Discover (v.) 發現
  * 指找到隱藏的、未知的或丟失的事物。名詞形式為 discovery。
  * The police were able to quickly discover what had happened to the man's lost wallet. (警察能夠迅速發現那個男人遺失的錢包發生了什麼事。)
  * Many students discover their lifelong passion for science under the guidance of a great teacher. (許多學生在優秀老師的指導下發現了自己對科學的終身熱愛。)
  * I discovered a tiny stain on my favorite shirt after I returned from the restaurant. (從餐廳回來後，我在我最喜歡的襯衫上發現了一個小污漬。)
* Ashamed (adj.) 感到羞恥的、慚愧的
  * 常用結構為 `be ashamed of`。強調對自己的行為感到內疚或丟臉。
  * Ashamed of his greedy behavior, Gary burst into tears and apologized to his parents. (對自己的貪婪行為感到羞恥，Gary 突然大哭並向父母道歉。)
  * You should be ashamed of yourself for treating a stranger so rudely. (你應該為自己對陌生人如此無理而感到羞恥。)
  * He felt ashamed that he couldn't fulfill his promise to help his sister with her homework. (他因為無法履行幫妹妹寫作業的諾言而感到羞愧。)
* Greedy (adj.) 貪婪的、貪心的
  * 名詞形式為 greed。指過度渴望金錢、食物或權力。
  * The greedy man wanted to keep all the cash for himself instead of returning the wallet. (那個貪婪的人想把所有的現金據為己有，而不是歸還錢包。)
  * Don't be greedy; leave some of those delicious chocolate tarts for the other guests. (別太貪心；留一些美味的巧克力塔給其他客人。)
  * Greed can sometimes blind a person's judgement and lead to very poor decisions. (貪婪有時會蒙蔽一個人的判斷力，並導致非常糟糕的決定。)
* Repay (v.) 償還、報答
  * 詞源來自 re- (再次) + pay (支付)。指還錢，或是報答某人的恩情。
  * Gary's parents repaid the money he had spent on the video game system. (Gary 的父母償還了他花在電玩系統上的錢。)
  * I will never be able to repay Alana for the unconditional love and support she gives me. (我永遠無法報答 Alana 給我的無條件的愛與支持。❤️)
  * The bank requires him to repay the loan within the next three years. (銀行要求他在未來三年內償還貸款。)
* Severe (adj.) 嚴厲的、嚴重的
  * 副詞形式為 severely。常用於描述天氣、疾病或懲罰的嚴重程度。
  * Gary's parents punished him severely to make sure he learned his lesson. (Gary 的父母嚴厲地懲罰了他，以確保他吸取了教訓。)
  * The city suffered from a severe water shortage due to the lack of rain this summer. (由於今年夏天缺雨，這座城市遭受了嚴重的缺水之苦。)
  * He received a severe warning from the police for his irresponsible behavior on the street. (他因為在街上的不負責任行為而收到了警察的嚴厲警告。)
* Apology (n.) 道歉
  * 動詞形式為 apologize。常用搭配為 `make an apology to sb` 或 `write a letter of apology`。
  * Gary's parents made him write a letter of apology to the man who lost his wallet. (Gary 的父母叫他寫一封道歉信給那位丟了錢包的男人。)
  * The company issued a public apology for the technical errors in their recent software update. (公司針對最近軟體更新中的技術錯誤發表了公開道歉。)
  * A sincere apology is often the first step toward repairing a broken relationship. (真誠的道歉通常是修復破損關係的第一步。)
